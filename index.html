Profile

Researcher, educator, and science communicator

My work combines developmental biology, molecular and cellular assays, microscopy, computational analysis, and science communication to understand how neural and vascular systems develop together.

Current training

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Biology at the University of North Dakota, advised by Dr. Diane Darland. My dissertation examines vascular-derived factors and their influence on the developing mouse cortex.

Scholarly tools

My work integrates single-cell and RNA-seq analysis, flow cytometry, immunolabeling, confocal microscopy, advanced imaging analysis, and experimental models of neural stem cell behavior.

Research Focus

How vascular signals shape cortical development

My research asks how vascular-derived molecules influence neural stem cell fate and early cortical development. I focus on developmental windows when the embryonic mouse cortex transitions from a simple neuroepithelial structure toward layered, vascularized tissue.

Neurovascular development

I investigate how neural and vascular systems coordinate during embryonic mouse cortical development, particularly during early periods of neural expansion, differentiation, and vascular investment.

Mouse cortex Neural stem cells Development

VEGF and leptin signaling

My dissertation examines the regulatory roles of Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor (VEGF) isoforms and leptin/leptin receptor signaling in neural stem cell fate and neurovascular interactions.

VEGF Leptin receptor Cell fate

Integrated experimental and computational analysis

I combine molecular assays, advanced imaging, flow cytometry, and transcriptomic approaches to connect cell behavior, tissue structure, and gene-expression patterns.
